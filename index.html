Belly Button Biodiversity Dashboard

Use the interactive charts below to explore the dataset

About this Dashboard:

Did you know you have an entire ecosystem living in your belly button? You do! Participants in this study were asked to swab their belly button and submit their *ecosystem* samples for analysis. Selecting a 'Test Subject ID No.' from the drop-down menu below will update the charts. Each subject has a variety of bacteria in their ecosystem and it's interesting to see how much more there are when none are destroyed with a wash. Have fun exploring!

Demographic Info

This bar chart shows the top 10 bacterial cultures for this particular subject. An "OTU" is an Operational Taxonomic Unit, and in this instance it's being used to measure an individual, species, genus, or class of belly button bacteria! Hover over each bar to see more info.

This gauge chart shows the averaage frequency that this particular subject washed their belly button each week. The more they washed, the less bacteria in their sample... the washing wiped out the belly button ecosystem!

This bubble chart shows all the OTUs for this subject and the size and color of the bubble indiates the volume of each particular cluster.
